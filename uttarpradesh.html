Discover the Heritage of Uttar Pradesh

From the Taj Mahal to Varanasi ghats, experience India's cultural heartland

Top Tourist Spots in Uttar Pradesh

Taj Mahal

World-famous white marble mausoleum built by Shah Jahan — a UNESCO wonder of love.

Best: Oct–Mar

Agra Fort

Massive red sandstone fort with palaces and mosques overlooking the Yamuna.

Best: Oct–Mar

Fatehpur Sikri

Historic Mughal capital built by Akbar with palaces, mosques and courtyards.

Best: Oct–Mar

Varanasi Ghats

Sacred ghats on the Ganges — spiritual heart with evening aarti ceremonies.

Best: Oct–Mar

Sarnath

Buddhist site where Lord Buddha gave his first sermon — stupas and monasteries.

Best: Oct–Mar

Lucknow Bara Imambara

Nawabi-era marvel, famous for its architecture and Bhulbhulaiya maze.

Best: Oct–Mar

Ayodhya

Ancient city of Lord Rama — major pilgrimage destination with temples & ghats.

Best: Oct–Mar

Mathura & Vrindavan

Birthplace of Krishna — colorful temples, music and grand Holi celebrations.

Best: Oct–Mar

Jhansi Fort

Stronghold of Rani Lakshmibai during the 1857 revolt — great hilltop views.

Best: Oct–Mar

Chitrakoot

Mythical Ramayana site with temples, forests and serene riverbanks.

Best: Sep–Feb

Dudhwa National Park

Wildlife reserve with tigers, elephants, rhinos and diverse birds.

Best: Oct–Jun

Prayagraj Sangam

Triveni confluence of Ganga, Yamuna & Saraswati — site of Kumbh Mela.

Best: Oct–Feb

Culture of Uttar Pradesh

UP is the cradle of Indian traditions — Kathak dance, Hindustani classical music, Urdu literature, and exquisite crafts like Chikankari embroidery. Festivals like Holi, Diwali and Kumbh Mela reflect its vibrant spirit.

Wedding Style

UP weddings mix grandeur with tradition — brides in zardosi lehengas, grooms in sherwanis, rituals with shehnai music, and feasts featuring rich Awadhi cuisine.

Major Crops

  • Wheat - Staple crop and widely cultivated
  • Sugarcane - Major cash crop
  • Rice - Important food grain
  • Pulses - Various pulses grown across the state
  • Oilseeds - Sunflower, mustard, and others

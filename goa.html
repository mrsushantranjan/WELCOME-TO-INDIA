Discover the Beaches & Heritage of Goa

From vibrant beaches to historic churches, Goa is where culture meets the sea.

Top 12 Tourist Places in Goa

Baga Beach

One of the most popular beaches for nightlife and water sports.

Basilica of Bom Jesus

UNESCO World Heritage site, famous for Baroque architecture.

Fort Aguada

Seventeenth-century Portuguese fort with sea views.

Dudhsagar Falls

Magnificent four-tiered waterfall on the Mandovi River.

Calangute Beach

Known as the 'Queen of Beaches' in Goa.

Se Cathedral

One of the largest churches in Asia.

Anjuna Beach

Famous for its trance parties and flea markets.

Chapora Fort

Iconic fort with panoramic views of Vagator Beach.

Mangeshi Temple

Famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Colva Beach

Longest beach in South Goa, known for white sand.

Shanta Durga Temple

Important Hindu pilgrimage site in Goa.

Palolem Beach

Picturesque beach with calm waters and palm trees.

Culture of Goa

Goa has a unique blend of Indian and Portuguese cultures with festivals like Carnival, traditional dances, and delicious seafood cuisine.

Wedding Style

Goan weddings are lively, often held by the beach with music, dancing, and a mix of Christian and Hindu traditions.

Major Crops

  • Coconut
  • Paddy (Rice)
  • Cashew
  • Spices
